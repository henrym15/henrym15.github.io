Analyzing the Current State of LA County Public Libraries
What does the current makeup of libraries look like for LA County?
Background

The importance of having a local public library cannot be understated. Even in our modern world, where our phones store all of the information we could ever possibly want to know, having a physical location is paramount to building equitable access to information for everyone. Not only does it promote accessibility, but the sense of community is something that cannot be replicated digitally.

Growing up, I was lucky enough to have lived near a public library. I would visit with my sister nearly every week, borrowing books, printing research papers for our school projects, and participating in community-led events. During my sophomore year of high school, I was actually fortunate enough to be on the teen council, where I was able to volunteer my time to beautifying the surrounding area and managing the circulation of new books.

That's all to say that while I primarily visited my library for the purposes of reading, the things I contributed to and gained experience in expanded far beyond that. A library isn't just a place to borrow books -- it can be a place to nurture creativity, pique academic interest, and develop an altruistic mindset.

As we regress further and further into our smartphones and mobile devices, we must still consider the positive net impact that a public library holds, not just for accessibility, but for the promotion of a scholarly and communal mindset for our youth.

With that in mind, how does the current public library landscape look for LA County?
